sisterveda

I loved working on this Friday test at my code school because it was a design challenge coming from an actual client - a friend of my teacher's starting an Ayurvedic business. This required me to work within the constrains of an actual client's specifications and utilize the content she provided. Challenge accepted! I decided to take a more modern spin on the design, focusing on clean lines, lots of white space, and using color to enhance and not overwhelm.

Wireframes and user flows

This being the first test in our Design course, we were to focus on creating low-fidelity wireframes for three screen sizes - mobile, tablet, and desktop - before transforming them into high-fidelity mock-ups. This allowed me to focus on the user experience and flow through the site before getting into the weeds of asset selection and placement.

Subtle color and depth

I was very happy with the final outcome of the design. The site being solely informational in purpose, with no need for specific functionality, I came up with a design that is straightforward and organized. I used colored bars to indicate to the user which page they are currently viewing and shadows and opacity to add depth and the illusion of layers.
