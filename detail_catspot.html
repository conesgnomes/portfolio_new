CatSpot Site Redesign

While I was interning at Sq1, a creative agency in Portland, Oregon, I was asked to take a look at the landing page for CatSpot Litter and create a secondary design to incorporate into an A/B test. CatSpot was experiencing a lower conversion rate for users purchasing their monthly subscription box than they wanted and wanted to explore some alternative design options to hopefully bring that number up. I utilized user data gathered through Hotjar, a user behavior tracking tool, to generate an alternate design which rearranged the information structure to bring more pertinent information higher up on the screen.

Reading heatmap data for smarter design

There are two heat map reports that Hotjar generates that I found useful for this project - the first shows what percent of users view which portions of your page, providing an idea of how deep into your site users are getting before exiting. The second report shows where users are clicking on your site - the higher the percentage, the closer to red those areas will be (I have circled these hot spots in white above). For CatSpot, I could tell that users were only spending their time in the upper half of the landing page and using the 'Shop Now' button within the 'How It Works' section more than any of the other CTA buttons, which is located within the upper half of the page.

Information where it can be most useful

Knowing that the majority of users were only ever reaching the top half of the site before exiting, and those that were clicking to purchase the product were doing so using the CTA that was also located within the upper portion of the screen, I created a design that moved the product's pricing details higher to better inform consumers of their options before making a purchase.
