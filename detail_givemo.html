givemo

This was the culminating group project for my JavaScript course at Epicodus, the coding school where I gained all of my web development chops. The idea I had for this project was to create an app that allowed users to find charities in Portland, Oregon by category and easily make a monetary donation to them. In a world ever more cluttered by information, this app would allow users to cut through the noise and easily find causes they believe in to support. My group built the app in Angular 4, stored our data in a Firebase database, and used SASS for the styling. Best viewed in mobile.

Design for usability first

I was on point for the design of the app, and first and foremost I wanted it to be as user-friendly as possible. Single sign-on with a user's Google account, leading to a dashboard screen displaying their favorite causes, how much they had previously donated to them, and an ability to donate again with the click of a button. On the charity search page, we created single click search functionality, allowing the user to simply select one or more charity categories, and a list of matching organizations would dynamically display instantly. One more click and you are on your way helping make a difference in mere minutes. See far left image for initial sketches and notes and final outcome center and right.

Living the gradient life

For the overall aesthetic of the app, I wanted to capitalize on the trend in using gradients and large buttons and iconography that I have been seeing in the web design community. Seeing as this was an app involving monetary contributions, I wanted green to be a primary color in the design - by placing yellow and blue on either ends of the gradient spectrum, their marriage in the middle resulted in a beautiful green.
