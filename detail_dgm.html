digital gaming museum

This design came out of a two-day project in my Design course. We were given a design brief for a fictional site from a classmate and required to create a design that hit all its marks. My partner and I were asked to create a site for a video game museum. We were provided a color palette and asked to avoid using trademarked video game characters in our design.

Working Through the Design Process

We approached this project as if it were a design for an actual client, which meant starting with consumer research, asking our classmates (and Google) about what they would want to see in a museum site, and building out personas and user stories. From there we created wireframes and built out the user flow through the site before creating high fidelity mock-ups.

Practice with iconography

Along with developing the design and aesthetic of the site, my partner and I also used this project as an opportunity to practice our iconography. We wanted to have a large hero image in the header of our site, however since the museum we were designing for was fictional and we had no actual photos of it, we decided to create arcade-inspired icons in Sketch instead - a game controller and an arcade machine. We also created the social media icons and a logo for the museum.
