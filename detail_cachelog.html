cachelog

This website design was my answer to one of the weekly tests in my Design course at Epicodus, the coding and design school I attended. The prompt I received was to redesign the user interface for an Angular application we had built a month prior during our JavaScript course. I selected to redesign an app I created (and since rebranded) called Cachelog, a geocache database for adventurers. See the image below for a before (left) and after of the landing page to see the change brought by the redesign.

Working with a theme and content

Geocaching is an activity requiring the use of maps, compasses, and helps you build upon your survival skills in a fun way. I decided to take those concepts to the extreme and create a UI with a military fatigue feel to it. I brought this to life largely through typography and a stark design and layout. The app I was redesigning was relatively barebones in its content, so the utilization of whitespace was a clever way to work around a lack of content.

Icon design

One of my favorite requirements of this project was the necessity to create our own custom icons and practice our iconography in Sketch. Keeping with the sparse design aesthetic I decided to establish, I knew my icons had to be subdued in nature as well. I worked on creating icons made up of simple shapes layered on top of and subtracted from one another, resulting in four icons - a map, a compass, a telescope, and a globe.
